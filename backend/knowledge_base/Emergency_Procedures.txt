CRITICAL EMERGENCY SCENARIOS:

SCENARIO 1: COMPLETE HVAC FAILURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Symptoms:
- No cooling/heating in entire facility
- All zones unresponsive
- System dashboard shows offline

Immediate Actions:
1. Call SES immediately: (617) 564-4800
2. Check main circuit breakers (don't reset if tripping)
3. Open emergency ticket in portal
4. Post signage if facility must close
5. Notify members if temperature unsafe
6. Contact regional manager
7. Request emergency technician dispatch

Expected Response:
- SES acknowledgment: Within 15 minutes
- Technician dispatch: Within 2 hours
- Temporary solution: Same day
- Full repair: 24-48 hours

SCENARIO 2: FIRE/SMOKE/BURNING SMELL
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Immediate Actions:
1. EVACUATE facility immediately if smoke visible
2. Call 911 first for fire/smoke
3. Turn off HVAC main power if safe
4. Use fire extinguisher only if trained
5. Call SES after emergency services: (617) 564-4800
6. Do not re-enter until cleared
7. Document for insurance

Do Not:
- Investigate source alone
- Attempt repairs
- Re-energize system
- Allow members in facility

SCENARIO 3: WATER LEAK/FLOODING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Immediate Actions:
1. Identify water source location
2. Shut off water if accessible
3. Move equipment/members away from water
4. Call SES: (617) 564-4800
5. Place caution signs/barriers
6. Document with photos
7. Call restoration company if major

Priorities:
- Member safety first
- Prevent electrical hazards
- Minimize equipment damage
- Document for insurance

SCENARIO 4: EXTREME TEMPERATURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Temperature > 85°F or < 60°F:

Actions:
1. Check SES dashboard for alerts
2. Verify thermostat settings
3. Call SES: (617) 564-4800
4. Open high-bay doors if safe
5. Set up temporary fans if available
6. Post member advisories
7. Consider facility closure if extreme

Member Safety:
- Monitor for heat exhaustion symptoms
- Provide extra water stations
- Reduce high-intensity activities
- Shorten operating hours if needed

SCENARIO 5: ELECTRICAL ISSUES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Symptoms:
- Breakers tripping repeatedly
- Sparks or arcing
- Lights flickering with HVAC
- Burning electrical smell

Immediate Actions:
1. Turn off affected breakers
2. Call SES immediately: (617) 564-4800
3. Evacuate area if sparking
4. Do not attempt repairs
5. Keep members away from equipment
6. Document observations
7. Wait for qualified technician
